Spotlight Interview

Featuring Sam Strapason, Elicit Consulting

1)Give us a general introduction: What year you graduated, undergraduate / schools attended, undergraduate major, cool/uncool places you've lived, where you are located now:

I graduated from Buffalo State College in May 2013 with a B.S. in Applied Mathematics and a Minor in Coaching. I then packed up and moved to Raleigh, where I graduated in 2014. I returned to my hometown Buffalo, NY for a week after graduation. Since my company was remote I had infinite options of where I could live. I grabbed a few bags with my most important things and moved to Santa Monica, California, just a few miles from the coast.

2)Tell us about your company and the team that you work with.

Elicit is a pioneer in the world of customer science and customer centricity. I am a part of the data science team, where we analyze data to discover insights about customers. We work with our marketing team to help build out the story about the customers and their journey.

3)What has been the most interesting project or problem you've tackled?

I have loved being a part of a team that builds a customer foundation, ourapproachto customer insights, fromthebottom up. We start with raw data tables and a plan of action on day one. Then by analyzing customer behavior and overlaying research and other data to answer the why about customer behaviors, we are able to create actionable segments rather quickly. Seeing the segments implemented and used in thebusiness is a very rewarding experience. After all, what good is insight without action?

4)List an online resource that you've found helpful at your job and a short description.

Well, the first resource that I will always go to are my co-workers. Everyone has different types of experience that really make our team special. When a few of us are stumped, the next best resource I've found,besidesGoogle, is Stack Overflow. Simply put, it's a question and answer site for programmers.

5)Since you've graduated from "The Program" and gotten your evenings and weekends back, what have you had more time to do?

Since I've moved to Santa Monica, I've found extra time to get into hiking, exploring the area, and traveling to new cities. I also have found time to pursue my interest in craft beer by going to tastings and learning more about the ingredients, tastes, and processes to make beer. My boyfriend and I are hoping to brew our first batch soon!

6)The smartphone is today's window to the soul- send us your favorite picture that you've taken with your phone (or digital camera / DSLR / polaroid, etc)

As I mentioned before, I've started to get into hiking. One of our biggest feats has been Mount Baldy (10,064 ft). Though we had poorly planned and started late, it led us to having incredible views before going through some nasty switchbacks in the dark (we'll have to talk about the whole story over drinks sometime). We all work with technology all day and have access to our phone and everyone we know 24/7. This picture reminds me that it's our stories and experiences that make us who we are as people. We need to get as close to the edge as possible in order to experience the most out of life, but also watch our step so we don't go over.
