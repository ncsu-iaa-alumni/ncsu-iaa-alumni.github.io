Alumnus Spotlight:
Darin Brown

Darin is from the class of 2013 and now works at Disney! Here's the scoop on what it's like to work at the most magical place in the world.

Darin's background
Disney CMR Modeling & Analytics Manager in Orlando since July 2015
M.S., Analytics, NC State University, 2013
M.S., Operations Research, Air Force Institute of Technology, 2001
B.S., Mechanical Engineering, University of Miami, 1997

1) I hear you're working at Disney... what are some of the initiatives that your team is working on there?
I work in the Performance Analytics Team of the Disney Customer Managed Relationship (CMR) division, which is similar to Customer Relationship Management (CRM) division at other corporations.

My work at Disney focuses on the direct marketing to consumers including emails. Our analytic team supports the direct mail campaign using experimental design to promote a trip to Disney World.

Direct mail costs money so you want to send it to the right people, you want to measure the effects of your campaign. Yes, we are. Experimental design. Also we have millions of customers in the database, but only a very small percentage of them will do a resort stay, so we’re trying to predict a rare event.

We email past guests with discounts and try to guess when they will come back. It’s a direct application of what we learned in the program.

I just finished some survival analysis looking at past guests that have stayed at a resort before and we have a binary definition of (have visited Disney, have not visited). Say you’ve been to Disneyland once, and I’ve never been, is there a difference between us, or people who have come once, twice, three times, etc.

2) Looking back, what was your favorite / relevant class?
By far, logistic regression and survival analysis because there’s so many business problems that can be solved by those two techniques. Now with the Disney job, more experimental design as well.

I actually still talk to Dr. LaBarr. I ask him questions over e-mail and he totally gets back to you.

3) Since you've graduated from "The Program" and gotten your evenings and weekends back, what have you had more time to do?
I’m pretty much a dad. We have five children now. We don’t go to Disney every weekend, but we’ve gone a bunch since July. I can go to the parks anytime except for the holidays. At the office for birthday lunches, our team will go eat lunch at like, Hollywood Studio or something.

Andrew Brown also works for Disney. I have met other NC State graduates down here in Orlando too. Disney really likes recruiting NC State Analytics graduates.

4) Your current employer aside, what did you think of the latest release of Episode VII: The Force Awakens (don't worry, it's off the record, but actually kind of on the record)?
Very entertaining and a good start to the rest of the trilogy. Nostalgic for us old dudes who remember seeing the other ones.

5) Did the office do anything special for the movie release?
We didn’t see anything special for Star Wars office-wise…

I know there was a Star Wars cruise promotion. There wasn’t much CRM work before the movie, but now that the movie is out, there’s more. With the story being a secret, they didn’t want to overly market the characters.

Now I’m designing an experiment for a Star Wars campaign going out, trying to find people that have an affinity for Star Wars.

Our group gets a derived variable for Star Wars affinity… Maybe on your myDisneyExperience account you said you liked Yoda or something. They’ll look at your shopping behavior, whatever we can derive from internet activity or if you flat out indicated it in a survey.

I’m going to test if our affinity variable even matters – our group didn’t develop it, another group does that. We can go in and tweak their definition.

Finding out about the guests is hard because Disney doesn’t want to pry, but they also want to know what you like. Disney has a lot of huge platforms (ESPN, Marvel, Pixar etc) so whenever people get accounts there we get that data. And 3rd party data sources as well.
